## UMP — why everything they tried failed

**The feed has no edges.** No ending signal ever fires, so the brain never
closes the loop — that's the "unconscious 40 minutes." Blockers fail because
they add a WALL, not an ENDING — and when the itch is strong you climb the wall
(disable the app, type the password). Meditation apps fail because they ask you
to REPLACE the itch with discipline. Nothing *finishes* the itch. Bonus
mechanism: the scroll-trance suppresses memory formation → "I don't remember
the last 8 years" (their words: digital dissociation).

## UMS — the game that hasn't materialized

Meet the neural path where it already lives: the same thumb-flick, the same
soft variable reward — but with **an edge**. A session that ends itself, where
the ENDING is the payoff (the imagery resolves into stillness), and that leaves
a **memory artifact** (what you found tonight persists — anti-dissociation).
It doesn't block the itch. It finishes it.

1. **Bottom of the Feed** ⭐ flagship — a feed-shaped game that ENDS. You swipe

3. **Silence Trainer** — a game where the reward is literally nothing happening,
   in expanding intervals; the imagery breathes with you. "For people who can't
   handle the silence." (Desire #5; hardest sell, most defensible.)
